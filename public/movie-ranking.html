THE BEST MOVIES ACCORDING TO DAVID

My top 3 movies of all time

The Woman King

This movie according to me teaches five virtues:
Courage and resilience are essential in the face of oppression and hardship.
Sisterhood and unity empower women to overcome seemingly insurmountable odds.
Challenging tradition is necessary to forge a more just and compassionate future.
Leadership is earned through integrity, strength, and service to others.
Freedom and identity are worth fighting for, even when it means defying powerful systems.

The Man Called God

My Five Key Lessons:
Revenge has costs-Kang‑ta’s single‑minded pursuit of vengeance against his parents’ killers ultimately isolates him, costing the life of someone he loves and reminding us that vindictive desire often consumes more than it heals
Even a “god‑like” hero is still human-Although he’s portrayed with superhuman skills and resources, Kang‑ta experiences pain, loss, and betrayal, illustrating that no one is truly invulnerable.
Love challenges purpose-His growing bond with Bo‑bae forces him to reconsider the morality of his revenge mission. Genuine connection can disrupt even the most firm convictions
Power and corruption link easily-As Kang‑ta infiltrates Han Kang‑suk’s empire, he confronts a world built on loyalty, ambition, and calculated violence. Even righteous warriors can confront moral decay when trapped in structures of power
Redemption demands sacrifice-Toward the end, Kang‑ta chooses moments of peace over continued conflict. His transformation suggests that redemption isn’t found in vengeance, but in embracing community, forgiveness, and living beyond hatred.

Minari (2020)

Reasons:
It's gentle, deeply human, and emotionally rich.
It explores identity, belonging, and family with warmth and honesty.
The performances (especially by Youn Yuh-jung and Steven Yeun) are heartfelt and authentic.
It shows resilience and love even in life’s quiet, difficult moments.
